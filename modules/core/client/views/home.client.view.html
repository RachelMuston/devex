What is GC DevEx?

The Government of Canada Developers Exchange (GC DevEx) (beta) is designed to enable developers and designers across Canada to co-create improved digital public engagement tools with public servants.

The Privy Council Office (PCO), in partnership with Treasury Board Secretariat (TBS) and the Province of British Columbia is launching a GC Dev Ex BETA procurement platform to post small scale contracts.

The DevEx is an open source platform based on the Province of British Columbia Developers’ Exchange project. The code is available online with a license that permits re-use. The BC code has been copied (forked) into a repository on GitHub and has been adapted to meet Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS Web Standards and the Official Languages Act.

Who is involved?

GC DevEx has been developed using a collaborative approach to establish a micro-procurement platform that involves the Privy Council Office (PCO), and Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS).

These partners are responsible for governance, policy, procurement, programming, and design. PCO and TBS will lead the development of this platform, to be used by all departments within the Government of Canada.

Why was it established?

The intent of the GC DevEx (beta) is to build-up a library of digital tools that makes it easy to share, adapt and reuse fit-for-purpose solutions for governments and citizens to engage online. Additional emphasis on open source technologies will encourage copying (forking) to further enhance value of improvement on a common set of tools, rather than mandatory use of a single tool or process.

By focusing on small scale contracts, we’ll enable government to do business with small to medium sized enterprises, civic technology community groups and entrepreneurs, in a way that adds value to the innovation industry. This initiative fulfills a commitment under Canada’s participation in the Open Government Partnership (OGP).

How is it managed?

A governance structure and terms and conditions for use of the site will be developed. In addition to the technical stack and the governance layer, the project will require usability testing and an accessibility review. The scope is to remain manageable in order to launch a minimum viable product (MVP) quickly and learn through the experience. Additional features may be considered after launch, depending on available resources.
